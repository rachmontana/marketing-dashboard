MARKETING SUMMARY & ANALYSIS — MAY 2026 | Full Month Final Results

OVERALL STANDING

May closed as a split-result month. The top of the funnel significantly overdelivered — Leads finished at 188% of goal and SQLs crossed at 103% — but that volume didn't convert into revenue at the expected rate. Marketing Sourced ACV closed at $135,320 against a $177,304 goal (76%), a $41,984 shortfall. Cohort blocks show SQO Rate and SQO-to-Win Rate, and those conversion rates tell a more nuanced story than raw volume alone. YTD ACV sits at approximately $741,000 against an annual goal of ~$2,004,546 — roughly 37% complete with 42% of the year elapsed, putting us about $95,000 behind annual pace heading into June.

WHAT THE FUNNEL IS TELLING US

Volume is not the problem. With 3,460 leads against a goal of 1,843 (188%), and SQLs and SQOs both within striking distance of goal, the issue is conversion efficiency in the back half of the funnel. Total Opps came in at 88% of goal (244 vs. 276), and won deals and ACV show the largest gaps. The SQO Rate and SQO-to-Win Rate data in the cohort sheet makes this visible at the segment level: some cohorts are converting opps to SQOs at well above goal rates while still missing ACV targets, meaning the deals that are closing are smaller than planned. The Opp-to-SQO conversion recovered to goal (70%) after April's dip to 52%, which is a positive signal — but the MDR team's overall opp contribution and the autobooking channel both need attention given the Season 2 rocks tracking.

COHORT BREAKDOWN

Outperformers — ProConnect, CCH, Drake, Intuit General

These four cohorts are all beating ACV goal by significant margins. CCH is running at 250% of ACV goal, Drake at 181%, Intuit General at 246%, and ProConnect at 171%. Their SQO-to-Win Rates are above or at goal, and ASP is holding. These segments are working — the messaging, targeting, and sales motion are aligned. The focus should be on protecting and scaling what's driving results here.

Underperformers — General Accounting, UltraTax CS, Lacerte/ProSeries

General Accounting remains the most significant gap — $14,358 ACV vs. a $27,180 goal (53%). The SQO Rate came in at 40% of goal (0.40 actual vs. 0.60 goal), meaning opps aren't qualifying at the expected rate. Only 1 won deal closed against a goal of 11. This is both a qualification and a close rate problem and needs a dedicated diagnostic before June pipeline builds on the same pattern.

UltraTax CS created strong opp volume and SQOs came in at 23 (close to the 23 goal after adjustments), but ASP ran below goal ($2,599 vs. $3,856), and won deals were at 4 vs. a goal of 14. This is primarily a close rate and deal size issue. Worth noting: the Season 2 rock targeting 183 UTCS SQOs is currently at 56 (31% of season goal, 20% through season — 11 points behind pace).

Lacerte/ProSeries has strong ASP ($2,933 vs. $2,297 goal) but only 8 of 24 goal won deals closed. High quality, low volume. There are likely in-flight deals that didn't close in May that should be a priority push in June.

The SmartRequestAI Opps rock is the most urgent flag in the rocks section. At 43 actual against a 260 expected pace figure and a 1,302 season goal, this is tracking well below where it needs to be. June needs a major push.

REVENUE SOURCES

Revenue source data through April shows Organic declining steeply from $112,467 in January to $27,243 in April — a 76% drop over four months. This is the top revenue source and the trend is concerning. Intuit revenue has been more stable. Paid Search and Webinar are also declining. Understanding what's driving the Organic decline (SEO, brand search, direct) and whether May continued the trend should be a priority conversation.

RECOMMENDED NEXT STEPS FOR JUNE

General Accounting — root cause diagnostic. One won deal out of 62 opps (9% close rate vs. a 36% implied goal rate) is the biggest single issue in the data. Pull every General Accounting opp created in May, identify where they stalled, and determine if this is a targeting, routing, or product fit problem before June pipeline follows the same pattern.

SmartRequestAI Opps — urgent ramp. At 43 opps against a 1,302 season goal, this rock is tracking to miss by a wide margin. The team needs clarity this week on what activities are expected to drive SRAI opps and whether the season goal is still achievable given May's output.

Lacerte/ProSeries pipeline acceleration. Strong ASP means deals here are worth more than average. Work with Sales on in-flight Lacerte/ProSeries opps from May that didn't close — even 6–8 additional won deals would materially improve June ACV.

UltraTax CS ASP investigation. Running $1,257 below ASP goal suggests either a deal mix shift toward smaller accounts or active discounting. Understanding the cause prevents it compounding through the rest of the season.

Organic revenue decline. Four straight months of significant decline in the top revenue source needs an owner and a plan. Identify whether this is an SEO, brand, or channel attribution issue before it becomes a larger problem in Q3.

Protect what's working. ProConnect, CCH, Drake, and Intuit General are all significantly outperforming. Document the specific campaigns, channels, and messaging driving these results and determine whether elements can be applied to the underperforming cohorts.

WATCH LIST FOR JUNE

Annual ACV pace requires approximately $183,000/month for the rest of the year to hit the $2,004,546 goal. May's $135,320 was well short of that pace. June needs to be a meaningful step-up month.

MDR team momentum (Bryan at 25 opps, Kenny at 17, Javier consistent) is a real asset — the LinkedIn MDR Outbound Activation rock (Bryan's Season 2 rock) should be a lever for General Accounting and Lacerte/ProSeries specifically.

The Autobooking SQO rock and the broader autobooking channel contributed 128 opps in May — monitoring whether this scales in June will indicate whether the Rachel/autobooking motion is on track for the 875 season goal.
